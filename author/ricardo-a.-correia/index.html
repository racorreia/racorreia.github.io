Ricardo A. Correia

Post-doctoral researcher in Conservation Geography

University of Helsinki (FI)

Ricardo Correia is a post-doctoral researcher in Conservation Geography at the University of Helsinki, Finland. He is a member of the Helsinki Lab of Interdisciplinary Conservation Science (HELICS) and a fellow of the Helsinki Institute for Sustainability Science (HELSUS). He his a conservation scientist and his research interests include, among others, the study of human-nature interactions in space and time, the use of digital data for biodiversity conservation, protected area management and values, and climate change impacts in natural environments. He is a meber of the Conservation Culturomics (ConsCult) Working Group steering committee, part of the Society for Conservation Biology, and a member of the well-being group at the Department of Geosciences and Geography of the University of Helsinki.
