Distribution and population trends of the European Roller in pseudo-steppe areas of Portugal: results from a census in sixteen SPAs and IBAs

Inês Catry, João Paulo Silva, Ana Cardoso, Ana Martins, Ana Delgado, Ana Rita Sanches, Andreia Santos, Beatriz Estanque, Carlos Miguel Cruz, Carlos Pacheco 10, Domingos Leitão, Eunice Pereira, Estrela Matilde, Fernando Moital, Fernando Romba, Nuno Sequeira, Paulo Monteiro, Pedro Rocha, Ricardo A. Correia, Rita Alcazar, Rogério Cangarato, Ruben Heleno, Teresa Catry, Teresa Silva, Tiago Ferro
October 2011
PDF
Image credit: Catry et al. 2011

Abstract

The Roller (Coracias garrulus) is one of the seventeen Portuguese breeding birds classified as Critically Endangered. Nonetheless, despite its conservation status, it has received very little attention from scientists and conservationists in Portugal. Its population size is unknown and there is limited information on the distribution and population trends of this species. Therefore, a large-scale assessment of the Roller population was carried out in 2009 in sixteen Special Protection Areas (SPAs) and Important Bird Areas (IBAs) with cereal-steppic characteristics in the Alentejo and Beira Baixa regions. During the breeding season, 1570 human made structures were surveyed in an area of more than 320 000 hectares in order to assess the occurrence and abundance of rollers and to study the selection of structures by breeding pairs. Overall, the Roller population was estimated at 57 to 62 breeding pairs, distributed in only six of the sixteen prospected areas. Castro Verde was the most important area with 52-55 pairs, followed by residual populations in Vila Fernando (2-3 pairs), Évora, S. Vicente and Cuba (1 pair) and Moura-Mourão-Barrancos (0-1 pair). Our results confirm the scarcity and highly fragmented distribution of the species, suggesting a general negative trend of the Portuguese population. The characterization of building structures in the surveyed areas suggests that the presence of the species might be constrained by the lack of suitable cavities for nesting. Rollers positively selected adobe-built structures surrounded by fallows. Only in Castro Verde the species is relatively abundant and is likely to be increasing as a result of the high availability of suitable breeding sites and high quality habitat. This study highlights the need for urgent intervention in order to protect this endangered species. The maintenance and restoration of the traditional extensive cereal cultivation in the surveyed SPAs and IBAs (threatened by agriculture intensification) and the provisioning of artificial nest-sites are likely the most effective measures to guarantee the species conservation.
